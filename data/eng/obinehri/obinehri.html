Ob River

Ob is one of the longest rivers in the Asian continent, formed by the confluence of Biya and Katun rivers and travels 3,650 km from its origin to its mouth at the Arctic Ocean. The Ob River basin spans 5,411 km with its longest arm Black Irtysh, covers 2,990,000 km, and the widest part is around 19 km. Starting from the south of Siberia and flowing into the Arctic Sea, the Ob River is very convenient for passenger and freight transportation. It is also suitable for vehicle crossing in winter when it is frozen. Ob is located within Russian terrain.
