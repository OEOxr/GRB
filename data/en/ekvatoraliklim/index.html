Equatorial Climate

Occurs in countries along the equatorial line. Countries in the equatorial climate zone do have a rain-free seasons. These are areas where there is constant rain annual precipitation is very high. About 60 mm of precipitation is seen every month. In the equatorial climate, there are no winter or summer months. The weather is warm and rainy throughout the year. Precipitation is regular and frequent. In the equatorial climate zone, the temperature difference between day and night is greater than the annual temperature difference. Countries where the equatorial climate is seen are Cameroon, Kenya, Union of Comoros, Madagascar, Uganda, Belize, Costa Rica, Dominican Republic, Guatemala, Honduras, Jamaica, Nicaragua, Panama, USA, Bolivia, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador, French Guiana, Guyana, Suriname, Venezuela, Indonesia, Brunei, Japan, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Australia, Papua New Guinea, Fiji, Samoa, Solomon Islands and Vanuatu.
