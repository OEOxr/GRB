Galapagos Islands

This is an archipelago of Ecuador 1,000 km west of the South American continent on the Pacific Ocean, with a total area of 50,000 km2. It consists of 14 large, 8 small and 40 even smaller islets. There are many volcanoes still active on the islands. The general geographical character is steep and rugged terrain formed by volcanoes, with craters and deep valleys. The volcanic islands formed by layers of lava accumulated over long periods of time are now home to many biological species specific to this region alone.
