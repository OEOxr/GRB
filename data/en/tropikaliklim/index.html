Tropical Climate

In tropical climates, according to the Köppen climate classification, a temperature below 18°C does not occur for 12 months of the year. Among grass patches growing in the tropical region drought-tolerant trees can be found. In a tropical climate, precipitation is higher at the regions where sunlight is vertical. The growth rate and lifespan of plants are also high in the tropical climate. Bamboo is a typical example. Tropical climate is the type of climate seen in tropical regions. Tropical regions are as follows: The area from Sahara to South Africa, all of southeast Asia, The Arabian Peninsula, Madagascar, Pacific Islands, Central America, central Mexico to central south America, northern Argentina, northern Chile, Indonesia, Philippines and Northern Australia. The unique character of the tropical climate is that the average temperature is high in all months of the year. The most famous tropical countries which are attractive in terms of tourism are the Maldives, Bora Bora Islands or Indonesia.
