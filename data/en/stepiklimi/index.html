Steppe Climate

Steppe climate, which is seen in the interiors of the Central Belt regions, is common at the interiors of Anatolia, Central Asia, central parts of North America and the Iranian Plateau. It is most common in the inner land sections of warm or temperate belts. Precipitation usually falls during the spring season. The summer months are quite dry, so the general vegetation is in the form of steppes. It is considered as a transition climate. The differences between the average annual temperatures are high.
