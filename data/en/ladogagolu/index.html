Lake Ladoga

It is the largest freshwater lake in Europe, located within the borders of Leningrad Province of the Russian Federation. Its surface area is 17,700 kmÂ², its length from north to south is 219 kilometers, its width is approximately 80 km, the deepest point is at 251 meters and the average depth is 50 meters. There are 660 islands in the lake with a total area of nearly 430 km2. Basin countries are Russia and Finland. Lake Ladoga basin spans an area of approximately 270,000 km2 and includes 50,000 lakes and 3,500 rivers. Ladoga lake, which feeds on the Svir, Volhov, Vuoksi and Syas rivers, is the only natural habitat of the endemic Ladoga Ringed Seal. The lake is also rich in terms of fish species and fishing is widely practiced. Thanks to Lake Ladoga, it is possible to reach the Baltic Sea from the Volga River.
