Borneo

Borneo, located at the north of Java Island in Indonesia, is the third largest island in the world. The island is shared by Brunei, Indonesia and Malaysia. It is the largest island in the Malay Archipelago with an area of 743,330 kmÂ². Mount Kinabalu is the highest point on the island with its peak at 4,095 m. Borneo has important cave systems, such as Clearwater Cave and Deer Cave. The rainforest in Borneo is 130 million years old. The island is home to 20,000 distinct species of animals and it has been the evolutionary habitat of many endemic species. Borneo Rainforest, the Borneo orangutan's only habitat, is also a refuge for many endemic forest dwellers. About 20 million people live on the island, most of them in coastal cities. The largest city on the island is Kuching, tied to Malaysia, with a population of 660,000.
