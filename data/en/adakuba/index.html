Cuba

Cuba is an island country in the Caribbean Sea with a total area of 110,860 km². The coastline of the island is 3,735 km long and there is a 29 km long border with the U.S. territory at Guantanamo Bay. Cuba is the largest country in the Caribbean region by land area. Coastal areas include both white sandy beaches and wetlands. The largest of the wetlands is Zapata Swamp with an area of 4,500 km². Cuba is an island rich in terms of mineral resources. It has the second largest nickel reserve in the world after Russia. In the past, sugarcane farming has played a significant role in Cuba's economy and it is still grown in large areas. Tropical climate prevails in Cuba and trade winds play a key role in shaping the region's climate. The island's dry season is from November to April, and the rainy season is from May to October.
