Lake Victoria

Victoria, the world's second largest freshwater lake and the largest lake in African, is located at the east of the continent, shared by Tanzania, Uganda and Kenya. The area of the lake that constitutes the main water supply of River Nile, is 68,000 km2. Its length from north to south is 337 km, the widest section is 240 km. The deepest point is at around 85 m and the average depth is approximately 40 m. The Great Rift Valley stretches across the lakes system which includes Lake Victoria. Ugandan cities Kampala and Entebbe are at the north coast of the lake. Emin Pasha Gulf is at the southwest and Speke Gulf is at the southeast coast. More than 200 species of fish live in the lake.
